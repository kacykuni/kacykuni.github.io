## Introduction
This semester in ICS 414, I worked on a forked version of PantryPals which was a different class project from the previous semester. Our project named Shelf Awareness focused on expanding functionality and improving the overall user experience. Coming into the course and having just finished ICS 314, I was pretty familiar with frontend development but had limited experience working with backend development and databases. This semester has definitely challenged me to step outside of my comfort zone with many struggles, particularly in backend development, database management, and team challenges. This class has given me more experience in adapting within a collaborative environment and deepened my understanding of full stack software development. Since then I have been able to strengthen my technical skills that have helped me grow into a more well-rounded developer.

## Initial Expectations 
My initial expectations for this class was that we would be developing a web application from scratch with features much more advanced than what was expected from my previous ICS 314 class. I was confident coming into this class with my fresh knowledge only to be put to the test as we began working on Pantry Pals. Since Pantry Pals was not originally built by me, we spent a significant amount of time on reviewing code, style, and functionality was definitely a challenge since noticing that each developer has their own coding styles, structures, and different approaches to implementing certain features that sometimes did not follow the coding standards that I understand. This was an ongoing challenge throughout the semester since finding some features that were only partially implemented or only a mockup of what it should be because we had to figure out what was necessary, what needed to be fixed, and how new functionality should be added to further improve the web application. This gave me real-world software development experience because as the semester progressed I realized how much patience was needed for this careful analysis and gave me a deep understanding on how to read, debug, and extend existing code/functionality rather than only working within the comfort of something that I created. 

## Technical Growth and Challenges
After becoming more familiar with the original codebase and making Shelf Awareness something of our own, I took on responsibilities in both frontend and backend development, and more leadership roles in trying to make our milestone goals clear and concise. Throughout the semester, I encountered several challenges, including removing Firebase implementations, resolving database inconsistencies, fixing duplicate migration issues, and restructuring the schema. These issues were mostly very time-consuming because they required me to not only debug and understand the root of the problem, but also explain the solutions to my teammates so they could fix their local environments. I had to think critically about how these issues occurred and identify steps to prevent them in the future. Furthermore since each of my teammates had different levels of experience, communication and collaboration were crucial to meet each milestone goal. I realized early on that development is rarely straightforward with many problems that weren’t inherently obvious. This forced me to be patient, adaptable, and proactive so that I can be more confident in troubleshooting when issues arise. 

## Key Takeaways
Overall, this course significantly strengthened my understanding of full-stack development within a collaborative environment. Throughout the semester, I made an effort to take on more leadership responsibilities, especially in areas where my teammates felt less confident, such as backend development. By stepping in to help troubleshoot issues, explain concepts, and share best practices, I learned the importance of clear communication and patience when working through complex problems as a team. I also realized how important organization and planning are in avoiding last-minute issues, which pushed me to be more proactive in assigning and completing tasks.

More importantly, this experience gave me a realistic view of what software development looks like outside of the classroom. Working with an unfamiliar codebase, dealing with incomplete features, and resolving unexpected technical challenges showed me that development is rarely straightforward. Through these challenges, I became more confident in my problem-solving abilities and more comfortable stepping outside of my comfort zone. Overall, this course has prepared me to approach future projects with greater confidence, adaptability, and a stronger foundation in both frontend and backend development.
